### Risk Treatment Options Framework

```markdown
# Risk Treatment Framework

## Treatment Strategy Selection

### Risk Avoidance
**When to Use:**
- Risk probability or impact is unacceptably high
- Cost of avoidance is less than potential loss
- Alternative approaches are available
- Risk conflicts with core business values

**Implementation Approaches:**
- Eliminate the risk source
- Change project scope or approach
- Select different technology or vendor
- Modify business processes
- Restructure organizational approach

**Example Applications:**
- Avoid high-risk geographic markets
- Eliminate unsecure payment methods
- Cancel risky third-party integrations
- Postpone features with regulatory uncertainty

### Risk Mitigation (Reduction)
**When to Use:**
- Risk cannot be completely avoided
- Reduction measures are cost-effective
- Residual risk is acceptable
- Multiple control options are available

**Control Types:**
1. **Preventive Controls**
   - Access controls and authentication
   - Input validation and sanitization
   - Segregation of duties
   - Authorization workflows
   - Network security measures

2. **Detective Controls**
   - Monitoring and alerting systems
   - Audit trails and logging
   - Fraud detection algorithms
   - Performance monitoring
   - Compliance scanning

3. **Corrective Controls**
   - Incident response procedures
   - Backup and recovery systems
   - Error correction processes
   - Escalation procedures
   - Business continuity plans

### Risk Transfer
**When to Use:**
- Risk impact exceeds organizational capacity
- Transfer cost is less than potential loss
- Specialized expertise is required
- Regulatory or contractual requirements exist

**Transfer Mechanisms:**
- Insurance policies and coverage
- Contractual risk allocation
- Outsourcing to specialists
- Indemnification agreements
- Performance bonds and guarantees

**Insurance Considerations:**
- Cyber liability insurance
- Errors and omissions coverage
- Directors and officers insurance
- General liability protection
- Business interruption coverage

### Risk Acceptance
**When to Use:**
- Risk is within acceptable tolerance
- Mitigation costs exceed potential benefits
- Risk probability is very low
- Organization has capacity to absorb impact

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- Explicit management approval
- Documented risk assessment
- Ongoing monitoring procedures
- Contingency plans prepared
- Regular review and reassessment
```

### Crisis Management and Response

```markdown
# Crisis Response Framework

## Crisis Classification Levels

### Level 1: Minor Incident
**Characteristics:**
- Limited impact on operations
- No regulatory implications
- Minimal customer impact
- Standard recovery procedures

**Response Team:** Department manager + immediate team
**Response Time:** 4 hours
**Communication:** Internal only

### Level 2: Significant Incident
**Characteristics:**
- Moderate operational impact
- Potential regulatory interest
- Customer service disruption
- Extended recovery time

**Response Team:** Senior management + cross-functional team
**Response Time:** 2 hours
**Communication:** Internal + key customers

### Level 3: Major Crisis
**Characteristics:**
- Severe operational disruption
- Regulatory reporting required
- Significant customer impact
- Complex recovery procedures

**Response Team:** Executive leadership + crisis team
**Response Time:** 1 hour
**Communication:** All stakeholders + media

## Crisis Response Procedures

### Immediate Response (0-2 hours)
1. **Crisis Assessment**
   - Confirm crisis occurrence
   - Assess initial impact and scope
   - Classify crisis level
   - Activate response team

2. **Containment Actions**
   - Implement immediate safeguards
   - Isolate affected systems
   - Preserve evidence and logs
   - Prevent further damage

3. **Stakeholder Notification**
   - Internal escalation
   - Customer communication
   - Regulatory reporting
   - Media response preparation

### Short-term Response (2-24 hours)
1. **Detailed Assessment**
   - Comprehensive impact analysis
   - Root cause investigation
   - Resource requirement evaluation
   - Recovery timeline estimation

2. **Recovery Implementation**
   - Execute recovery procedures
   - Monitor progress and effectiveness
   - Adjust strategies as needed
   - Coordinate with external parties

3. **Communication Management**
   - Regular stakeholder updates
   - Media relations
   - Customer service support
   - Regulatory liaison

### Long-term Response (24+ hours)
1. **Complete Recovery**
   - Full system restoration
   - Comprehensive testing
   - Business operation normalization
   - Performance monitoring

2. **Post-Crisis Analysis**
   - Lessons learned documentation
   - Process improvement identification
   - Control enhancement recommendations
   - Crisis response evaluation

3. **Prevention and Preparedness**
   - Risk assessment updates
   - Control implementation
   - Training and awareness
   - Crisis plan refinement
```
